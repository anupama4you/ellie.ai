AI Receptionist vs Hiring a Receptionist: The Real Comparison for Australian Businesses

If your business is missing calls, you're facing a familiar choice: hire a receptionist, or explore an AI alternative. Both options solve the problem in different ways, at very different price points. This article breaks down the real costs, capabilities, and trade-offs so you can make an informed decision.

The True Cost of Hiring a Receptionist in Australia

A full-time receptionist in Australia typically earns between $55,000 and $70,000 per year in base salary, depending on the city and industry. But salary is only part of the picture. When you add superannuation (11.5% from 2024), leave entitlements, workers compensation insurance, payroll tax, and the time spent recruiting and training, the real cost of a full-time hire is often closer to $75,000 to $90,000 per year.

Part-time arrangements reduce that figure, but they introduce gaps. A part-time receptionist working school hours won't answer calls at 7am from a tradie wanting to book in, or at 6pm from a patient trying to reschedule an appointment. Those calls don't disappear. Most callers who reach voicemail simply don't call back, which means missed revenue every single time.

There's also the human factor. Staff take sick days, go on holidays, and sometimes resign at the worst possible moment. Every gap in coverage is a gap in your business.

What an AI Receptionist Actually Does

An AI receptionist handles inbound calls using voice technology trained on your business. It greets callers, answers common questions, books appointments, and sends SMS confirmations, all without any human involvement. It works around the clock, including weekends and public holidays, and never calls in sick.

The capabilities vary between providers, but a well-built AI receptionist can manage a surprisingly wide range of calls. Where it differs from a human is in handling genuinely complex or emotionally sensitive conversations. If a caller is distressed, confused, or has a highly unusual query, a human receptionist will generally respond with more nuance.

That said, for the majority of calls most Australian businesses receive, including appointment bookings, pricing enquiries, directions, and hours of operation, an AI receptionist handles the job effectively and consistently.

Cost Comparison: AI vs Human

The cost difference is significant. AI receptionist services in Australia typically start from around $199 AUD per month. Even at higher tiers with more features, most businesses spend well under $5,000 per year. Compare that to the $75,000-plus annual cost of a full-time human receptionist, and the financial case for AI is hard to ignore for call-handling tasks alone.

For a more detailed breakdown of what you can expect to pay across different service types, the virtual receptionist cost guide for Australia is worth a look before you commit to anything.

The relevant question isn't just cost, though. It's whether an AI can do enough of the job well enough that the cost difference is justified. For many businesses, the answer is yes. For others, a hybrid approach works better: an AI receptionist handles routine calls and after-hours enquiries, while a human staff member manages complex client relationships during business hours.

Where a Human Receptionist Still Wins

There are scenarios where a human receptionist genuinely adds more value than any AI can currently replicate.

  • High-stakes client relationships: If your business handles sensitive matters, such as legal, medical, or financial services where callers are often stressed or vulnerable, a human voice with genuine empathy matters.
  • Complex multi-step tasks: If your receptionist spends significant time coordinating between multiple staff, managing complex schedules, or processing paperwork, AI tools cover only a portion of that role.
  • Front-of-house presence: If you have a physical reception desk where clients walk in, you need a real person there regardless of how calls are handled.
  • Brand-sensitive first impressions: Some businesses in premium service categories want a highly personalised, bespoke experience from the very first interaction.

In these cases, a human receptionist is a genuine business investment. The question is whether you need that level of service for every call, or whether AI can shoulder the routine workload so your staff can focus on higher-value interactions.

Which Option Is Right for Your Business?

Here's a practical way to think about it. If you're a small to medium business where the majority of inbound calls are bookings, enquiries, or requests for basic information, an AI receptionist will likely handle 80 to 90 per cent of your call volume effectively, at a fraction of the cost of a human hire.

If you run a larger operation with complex front-desk responsibilities, a hybrid model often makes the most sense: AI for after-hours and overflow, humans for relationship-intensive work during business hours.
