How Much Does a Virtual Receptionist Cost in Australia?

If you're a business owner researching phone answering options, you've probably noticed that pricing is all over the place. Some services advertise low entry rates, then hit you with per-minute charges. Others bundle everything into a flat monthly fee. And now AI receptionists are entering the mix with a completely different cost structure.

This guide breaks down what you'll actually pay for virtual receptionist services in Australia, what drives those costs, and how to work out which model makes sense for your business.

The Main Types of Virtual Receptionist Services

Before comparing prices, it helps to understand what you're actually comparing. Virtual receptionist services in Australia generally fall into three categories.

  • Live answering services: Real humans answer your calls, usually from a shared call centre. You pay for their time.
  • Offshore answering services: Similar to live answering, but staff are based overseas. Lower cost, but often with trade-offs in accent, local knowledge, and reliability.
  • AI receptionists: Software answers calls automatically, handles common enquiries, books appointments, and sends confirmations. No humans involved.

Each has a different pricing model, and the right choice depends on your call volume, the complexity of your enquiries, and what you can afford each month.

What Does a Live Virtual Receptionist Cost in Australia?

Live Australian-based answering services typically charge in one of two ways: a per-minute rate or a monthly package based on call volume.

Per-minute pricing usually sits between $1.50 and $3.50 per minute for Australian-based operators. A three-minute call costs you between $4.50 and $10.50. If you receive 100 calls a month at that rate, you're looking at $450 to $1,050 per month before any extras.

Monthly packages are more predictable. Entry-level packages for small businesses typically start around $100 to $150 AUD per month, but these often cover only a small number of calls or minutes. Once you exceed your allocation, you pay per-minute overage charges, which can push your bill significantly higher.

More comprehensive packages with dedicated or semi-dedicated receptionists can run from $500 to over $2,000 per month, depending on hours of coverage and call complexity.

What About Offshore Answering Services?

Offshore services, often based in the Philippines or other parts of Southeast Asia, can cost considerably less, sometimes 40 to 60 per cent cheaper than Australian-based equivalents. Entry packages might start at $60 to $100 AUD per month.

The trade-off is that callers will notice the difference. For some businesses this doesn't matter much. For others, particularly those in professional services, healthcare, or trades where trust and local knowledge matter, it can affect how your business is perceived. It's worth testing before committing.

What Does an AI Receptionist Cost in Australia?

AI receptionists represent a newer category, and their pricing is structured differently. Because there's no labour cost per call, AI services typically charge a flat monthly subscription rather than a per-minute rate.

This is a meaningful distinction. With a live service, a busy month means a bigger bill. With an AI receptionist, your costs stay consistent regardless of how many calls you receive.

Hidden Costs to Watch For

Whatever type of service you're considering, ask about these before signing up.

  • Setup fees: Some providers charge $100 to $500 to onboard your business. Others include setup at no charge.
  • Overage charges: Per-minute or per-call charges above your monthly allocation can make your bill unpredictable.
  • Contract lock-in: Some services require 6 or 12 month contracts. If the service underperforms, you're stuck.
  • After-hours surcharges: Some live services charge more for calls outside business hours or on weekends. With AI, after-hours is typically included.
  • SMS and message delivery fees: Some providers charge separately for sending messages or notifications to you or your callers.

Always ask for a sample invoice or a clear breakdown of what's included before committing.

How to Work Out What Makes Sense for Your Business

The right service depends on three things: your call volume, the nature of your calls, and your budget.

If you receive fewer than 50 calls a month and most are straightforward, a basic live answering package or an AI receptionist will both work well. The AI option will likely be more cost-effective and available around the clock.

If you receive 100 to 300 calls a month and your enquiries follow a predictable pattern (booking appointments, answering common questions, taking messages), an AI receptionist will almost certainly be cheaper than a live service at comparable quality. The maths favour flat-fee pricing at that volume.

If your calls are highly complex, require negotiation, or involve sensitive conversations, a human is still the better choice, even at the higher cost.

It's also worth considering what happens when no one answers. Industry research consistently suggests that most callers who reach voicemail do not call back. Every missed call is a potential lost customer. When weighing up the cost of a receptionist service, factor in what missed calls are costing you now.
